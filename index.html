What is Art Therapy?

Art therapy is a professional, integrative mental health service that uses active art-making, the creative process, and applied psychological theory. It bridges our inner felt-sensations and our outward expression.

"You do not need to be an 'artist' to benefit. There is no judgment here. The target is never the final aesthetic output, but the therapeutic self-discovery found along the way."

Through tactile materials like paint, clay, collage, or sketching, we can bypass logical speech barriers to directly touch and heal complex, deep-seated emotional states.

Non-Verbal Release

Hello, I am Beverly. I am a certified art therapist graduated from the Vancouver Art Therapy Institute. I currently practice as a professional member of the Canadian Art Therapy Association (CATA) and the Hong Kong Association of Art Therapists (HKAAT). I also hold registered teacher qualifications in British Columbia, Canada, and Hong Kong.

My professional journey weaves a deep background in School & Community Psychology, Fine Arts, and Child Care with therapeutic practice. This allows me to foster a holistic, comprehensive approach to mental health.

Throughout my years supporting diverse individuals—from those facing neurological changes and learning challenges, to those battling acute illnesses or personal grief—I have consistently witnessed how each person's unique story holds a magnificent power to heal, to connect, and to influence others.
